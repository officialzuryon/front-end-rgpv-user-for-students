2.
a) Explain the following:
  1. Pneumatic

    Pneumatic sensors detect the absence/presence of an object or level using variations in air pressure. They are used when electrical sensors are unsuitable for the environment, for example, in the presence of flammable materials. They are often used to detect small or lightweight parts.

  2. Sensor features

    The various features related to sensors are as follows:

    1. Accuracy: It refers to the deviation of the measured value from the actual value.
    2. Sensitivity: It is the ratio of the change in output to the change in input.
    3. Resolution: It is the smallest change in the input that can be detected by a sensor.
    4. Drift: It is the variation in the sensor output when the input is constant.
    5. Response time: It refers to the time taken by the sensor to produce the output.
    6. Range: It is the limits between which the input can vary.
    7. Reliability: It is the ability of a sensor to perform its intended function without failure.
    8. Repeatability: It is the ability of a sensor to give the same output under repeated input conditions.
