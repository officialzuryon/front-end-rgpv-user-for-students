RGPV Grading System 2026 Explained: How to Calculate Your CGPA & SGPA

✍️ Team Admin  ·  📅 2026-03-28T12:55:56.332Z
Introduction If you are a student at Rajiv Gandhi Proudyogiki Vishwavidyalaya (RGPV) or one of its affiliated colleges across Madhya Pradesh, two numbers follow you everywhere — your SGPA and your CGPA. Whether you are applying for campus placements, filling out a GATE form, or just checking if you have cleared a tough semester, these figures define your academic profile. In this guide, we cover the official 2026 RGPV grading scale, the exact CGPA to percentage conversion formula, passing marks criteria, and step-by-step calculation examples — all in one place. The Official RGPV CGPA to Percentage Formula (2026) This is the single most important formula for every RGPV student. When applying for off-campus jobs at companies like TCS, Infosys, or Cognizant, or filling out forms for MBA/M.Tech entrances like CAT or GATE, you will be asked for a percentage — not a CGPA. Do not use random online converters. RGPV has one strict, official formula that applies to all B.Tech, M.Tech, MCA, and Diploma students: Percentage = (CGPA / 10) × 100 Example: If your final CGPA is 8.4, your official percentage is (8.4 / 10) × 100 = 84.0%. Need an Official Certificate? If a company or foreign university demands proof of this conversion, you can apply for an official CGPA Conversion Certificate via the RGPV Examination section by paying a nominal fee of INR 200 to 500. RGPV Passing Marks Criteria: What Do You Actually Need? To clear a subject and avoid a backlog, you must meet the passing threshold for theory and practical exams separately. Theory Exams: For a standard 70-mark theory paper, the minimum passing score is 22 marks. Score 21 and you will be awarded an 'F' grade. Practical Exams: Your practical marks are determined by the external examiner's viva and your HOD's assessment of your attendance and lab files. You must secure a passing grade independently in the practical section. The RGPV Grading Scale (2026 Updated) RGPV follows a credit-based grading model. Instead of raw marks out of 100, the university assigns a Grade and a corresponding Grade Point (ranging from 0 to 10) based on the percentage bracket your marks fall into. This scale applies to both your theoretical end-semester exams and your practical assessments. Grade | Marks Percentage Range | Grade Point | Performance Description A+ | 91% – 100% | 10 | Outstanding A | 81% – 90% | 9 | Excellent B+ | 71% – 80% | 8 | Very Good B | 61% – 70% | 7 | Good C | 51% – 60% | 6 | Average C+ | 41% – 50% | 5 | Satisfactory D | 31% – 40% | 4 | Marginal (Borderline Pass) F | 30% and Below | 0 | Fail I/W | N/A | 0 | Incomplete / Withdrawal Pro Tip for 2026 Students: Securing a grade of B+ or higher in core subjects is highly recommended if you are targeting top-tier IT service companies during campus placements. What is SGPA? (Semester Grade Point Average) Your SGPA measures your academic performance during one specific semester. Because some subjects are harder or more important than others, RGPV assigns different Credits to different subjects — for example, Mathematics might carry 4 credits while a minor lab carries 2. How to calculate SGPA: Multiply the Grade Point you earned in a subject by the Credits assigned to that subject. This gives you the Weighted Grade Points. Add all the Weighted Grade Points together for that semester. Divide that total by the total number of credits in the semester. Example Calculation — Imagine three subjects in Semester 1: Subject 1 (4 Credits): You score a B+ (Grade Point = 8). Weighted = 4 × 8 = 32 Subject 2 (3 Credits): You score an A (Grade Point = 9). Weighted = 3 × 9 = 27 Subject 3 (2 Credits): You score a C (Grade Point = 6). Weighted = 2 × 6 = 12 Total Weighted Points = 32 + 27 + 12 = 71 Total Credits = 4 + 3 + 2 = 9 Your SGPA = 71 / 9 = 7.88 What is CGPA? (Cumulative Grade Point Average) While SGPA shows your performance in a single semester, your CGPA is the average of your performance across all completed semesters — and it is the final number that appears on your degree certificate. To calculate your CGPA, RGPV takes the total weighted grade points across all your completed semesters and divides it by the total number of credits you have taken since your first year. Minimum CGPA Requirement: To officially earn a B.Tech or Diploma degree from RGPV, you must maintain a minimum overall CGPA of 5.0. If your final CGPA falls below this, you will not be awarded your degree, even if you have cleared all individual papers. Conclusion Your CGPA and the 22-mark threshold are the two numbers that determine your RGPV academic standing.
