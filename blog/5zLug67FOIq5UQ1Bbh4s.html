How to Avoid an ATKT in RGPV: A Survival Guide

✍️ Ansh Sakalley  ·  📅 2026-03-15T06:51:45.846Z
Getting an ATKT (Allowed To Keep Term) or a "backlog" is every RGPV student's nightmare. It adds stress to the next semester and can complicate your placement prospects. Fortunately, avoiding an ATKT is highly achievable with the right approach. 1. Never Leave a Question Blank: RGPV marking emphasizes attempts. Even if you only know a fraction of the answer, write it down. Draw a relevant diagram if you can't remember the exact text. 2. Target the Passing Marks First: Out of 70, you need 22 to pass. Focus your initial study time on mastering 3 out of the 5 units completely. This guarantees you can attempt enough questions to cross the passing threshold. 3. Use Shivani/Quantum Wisely: Local guidebooks are great for last-minute revision, but they often contain errors. Cross-verify important definitions with standard textbooks or your lecture notes. 4. The PYQ Lifeline: If you are short on time, literally just memorize the answers to the last 4 years of PYQs. It is the most reliable way to secure passing marks.
